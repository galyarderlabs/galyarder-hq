# Galyarder Current Gaps and Hiring Plan

## Purpose
This document describes the current situation of Galyarder as a digital company and defines where the next "hires" should happen.

Here, a hire can mean:
- adding a new agent
- importing a strong external skill
- replacing a weak skill
- formalizing a department workflow
- strengthening reporting to Linear or Obsidian

## Current Situation
### What Is Strong
Galyarder already has:
- strong workflow law
- a strong engineering and execution identity
- good orchestration DNA
- real agent differentiation
- a growing founder office layer
- early Obsidian integration

### What Is Weak
Galyarder is still uneven in:
- explicit org structure
- documented agent-to-skill mappings
- founder-facing reporting loops
- modular domain manifests
- competitive breadth in some departments compared with larger skill libraries
- consistent department-level docs

## Hiring Philosophy
Do not hire for vanity.
Hire only when the new skill or agent makes a department stronger.

Questions to ask before hiring:
- what problem does this solve?
- which department does it strengthen?
- who manages it?
- does it improve the founder's leverage?
- does it improve reporting, quality, or execution speed?

## Priority Hiring Areas
### 1. Engineering Department
Reason:
This is the highest-risk department if underpowered, and external repos currently have deeper engineering skill breadth.

Needs:
- stronger architecture review packs
- stronger CI/CD and observability packs
- stronger advanced code review / adversarial review
- stronger test automation / Playwright depth
- stronger database and migration design packs

Potential source inspiration:
- `claude-skills` engineering and engineering-team domains

### 2. Growth Department
Reason:
Growth still risks being a collection of marketing skills rather than a clear department with operator depth.

Needs:
- demand generation systems
- campaign analytics
- stronger lifecycle / churn packs
- content production systems
- better founder-brand to growth linkage

Potential source inspiration:
- `claude-skills` marketing-skill domain

### 3. Founder Office / Capital
Reason:
This has begun well, but it still needs formal reporting loops and cleaner operating structure.

Needs:
- board reporting templates
- investor pipeline operational templates
- standard founder office dashboards
- clear recurring outputs into Obsidian

### 4. Operations / Infrastructure
Reason:
There is room for better release, incident, infra, and observability depth.

Needs:
- incident commander style workflows
- release readiness automation
- observability and SLO practices
- infra design playbooks

### 5. Knowledge / Reporting
Reason:
Obsidian is present, but not yet formalized as the reporting destination for the digital company.

Needs:
- founder report templates
- department snapshot templates
- milestone summary templates
- decision log standards

## Department-Level Work To Define
Each department should eventually have:
- a lead agent
- a mapped worker-skill stack
- a Linear workflow pattern
- an Obsidian reporting pattern
- quality gates
- handoff rules to adjacent departments

## Reporting Gap
The biggest current systems gap is not just skill count.
It is reporting structure.

The digital company should produce consistent founder-readable outputs.
That means department heads should eventually know how to produce:
- weekly summaries
- milestone updates
- blockers and risks
- decision requests
- next actions

Those should roll up through `galyarder-specialist` and be preserved in Obsidian.

## Immediate Recommendations
1. Audit engineering skills against `claude-skills` first.
2. Formalize Obsidian report templates for every department.
3. Define a standard Linear structure for department execution.
4. Keep founder office as an optional expansion layer, not core law.
5. Rewrite more docs to reflect the digital company model.

## Decision Rule
When in doubt, hire to strengthen a department.
Do not hire just to increase the number of skills.

The metric is not library size.
The metric is whether the founder gains a more capable digital company.
