# Galyarder Knowledge Audit - Claude-Skills Benchmark

## Purpose
Audit the knowledge, documentation, and institutional memory layer of Galyarder using the best reusable patterns visible in `claude-skills`.

## Current Situation
This is one of the most strategically important departments for Galyarder because the solo founder needs memory, narrative continuity, and report quality.

Current strengths:
- Obsidian is already positioned as the memory and reporting system
- Linear is positioned as the task system of record
- founder reporting templates already exist

Current gaps:
- knowledge capture is still mostly manual
- cross-project memory quality is inconsistent
- department outputs are not yet normalized into a founder-readable archive
- decisions, milestones, and learnings are not fully linked into one knowledge graph

## Benchmark Patterns Worth Borrowing
### 1. Decision and Narrative Management
Potential hires or adaptations:
- `decision-logger`
- `internal-narrative`
- `board-deck-builder`
- `company-os`

Why this matters:
Knowledge is not just storage. It is the ability to explain why decisions were made and what changed over time.

### 2. Documentation Systems
Potential hires or adaptations:
- `process-docs`
- `runbook-generator`
- `quality-documentation-manager`
- `spec-driven-workflow`

Why this matters:
A digital company becomes much more valuable when its employees produce durable documents instead of disposable chat output.

### 3. Knowledge Architecture and Founder Reporting
Potential hires or adaptations:
- Obsidian-style workflow adaptations
- `milestone-summary`
- `decision-log`
- `board-update`

Why this matters:
The founder should be able to audit company state from Obsidian without manually stitching everything together.
