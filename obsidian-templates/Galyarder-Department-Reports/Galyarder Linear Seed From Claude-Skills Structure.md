# Galyarder Linear Seed From Claude-Skills Structure

## Purpose
This note captures what can be borrowed from the cloned `claude-skills` repository as a starting structure for Linear planning.

The point is not to copy their repo blindly.
The point is to use their domain segmentation as a seed for our own departmental rollout and audit plan.

## Observed Domain Structure In Claude-Skills
The cloned repo is strongly segmented by domain. Useful buckets observed:
- engineering
- engineering-team
- marketing-skill
- product-team
- project-management
- business-growth
- finance
- c-level-advisor
- ra-qm-team
- personas / agents
- commands

## How To Translate That Into Galyarder Linear Seeds
### 1. Engineering Audit Seed
Use their engineering + engineering-team split as inspiration for a two-level audit:
- core engineering skills
- advanced / specialized engineering skills

Potential Linear projects:
- Engineering Core Audit
- Engineering Advanced Audit
- Testing And Playwright Audit
- Review And Adversarial Audit
- CI/CD And Observability Audit

### 2. Growth Audit Seed
Their marketing domain is broad and segmented enough to inspire separate Linear workstreams:
- acquisition and demand
- CRO and funnel optimization
- SEO and content
- social and distribution
- analytics and tracking

Potential Linear projects:
- Growth Acquisition Audit
- CRO Audit
- Content And SEO Audit
- Social Distribution Audit
- Growth Analytics Audit

### 3. Product And PM Seed
They split product and project-management separately.
That is useful for Galyarder too.

Potential Linear projects:
- Product Department Mapping
- Project / Planning Workflow Audit
- Product Research And Strategy Audit

### 4. Founder / Executive Seed
Their c-level and founder-oriented structures are useful inspiration for our founder-office buildout.

Potential Linear projects:
- Founder Office Expansion
- Executive Reporting System
- Board / Investor Reporting System

### 5. Finance / Compliance Seed
Their finance and RA/QM segmentation suggests that finance and compliance should not be left fuzzy.

Potential Linear projects:
- Finance Department Audit
- Compliance / Legal Audit

## Proposed Linear Team / Project Seeding
If starting light, seed these first:
- Founder Office
- Engineering
- Growth
- Knowledge

If going fuller later:
- Product
- Security
- Operations
- Legal-Finance

## Rule
Borrow the structure.
Do not inherit the repo's logic blindly.
Every borrowed structure must still answer:
- which Galyarder department does this strengthen?
- which agent owns it?
- which founder-facing output improves because of it?
